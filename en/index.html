IT Center Ghukasavan

Harmonious development of Armenia requires faster development of village communities. Sharing your Business English and IT skills can give a chance to a generation of youth in Ghukasavan village & nearby communities.


About creation IT Center Ghukasanvan

On February 23rd, 2018, a Signing Ceremony of “The Project for Establishing the IT Vocational Center in Ghukasavan Village” within the frameworks of Assistance for Grassroots Human Security Projects was held at Village of Ararat Province. The ceremony was attended by Eiji Taguchi (Ambassador of Japan to Armenia) as well as Mr. Ara Vardanyan (Head of Ghukasanvan Community Municipality), Mr. Levon Mkrtchyan (Minister of Education and Science), Mr. Samvel Farmanyan (Deputy of the National Assembly, Head of the RA NA Armenia-Japan Friendship Group), Mr. Aghvan Vardanyan (Deputy of the National Assembly), Mr. Aramayis Grigoryan (Governor of Ararat Province) and other guests.

The project aims to provide vocational training to youth in Ghukasavan Village as well as its surrounding communities where over 3000 youth reside under high poverty and unemployment rate, by establishing the IT Vocational Center in the village. The Japanese Government will assist for constructing the building and fully equip the building with IT devices. The project is collaborated with “Leadership School Armenia” who has designed the concept and will provide substantial education program to the center.

About Programming lessons

In programming lessons, we learn programming languages and use them to create web pages. We learn different languages that have different levels of difficulty for each person During the 8 months of study, the students of the IT Center Ghukasavan gained enough knowledge to make a clone of popular websites such as Instagram, Facebook, Youtube.
